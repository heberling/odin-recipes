Buddha Bowl (vegan)

Description

This recipe shows you a very easy to make bowl thats vegan and perfect for a fast summer evening dinner or lunch!

You can exchange the recipes ingredients as you like. This recipe uses vegan ingredients and is targeted to an asian taste and topping it out with a creamy avocado sauce.

Here are some basic ideas that you can follow when making a bowl:

Choose a main ingeriendt

First you should choose a main ingredient that gives your meal a main direction of taste. Normally the main ingredient is a carbonhydrate food like noodles, potatoes or rice. You can also go a little bit crazy with this by choosing main ingredients like buckwheat or lentils.

Choose you side ingredient

Based on your main ingredient, you now choose your additional ingredients. Normally, when you are making a savoury dish, you use a lot of vegetables or Tofu. If you do not want to make a fully vegan bowl, use meat or dairy products as you like them. Keep in mind the rough direction that you set with the main ingredient.

Choose Spices

Now you are going to choose some spice that will fully commit you recipe to a flavour. If you choose some ingredients that complement an asian bowl, you can go for spices like Kurkuma, Curry or Garam Masala.

Choose a sauce

To finish you bowl, choose a sauce that underlines your flavour. For example, if you choose a asian-style bowl, you could create an easy but tasty creamy peanut sauce. You could also go with Guacamole or BBQ-Sauce.

Ingredients

This recipe is targeted towards 1 serving

    Main ingredients

  • 50g rice noodles
  • 1/2 Avocados
  • 50 carrots
  • 50 white and red cabbage
  • 1/2 Zucchini
  • 5 mini roma tomatoes
  • 1 can Chickpeas

    • Spices

  • salt
  • sesame
  • pepper
  • smoked paprika
  • cayenne pepper

    • for the sauce:

  • 1/2 Avocados
  • 50g Soy yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • 50ml water or vegetable milk

Steps

Here are the required steps to create this delicious meal:

  1. Cook the rice noodles as descripted on its packaging.
  2. Rinse the chickpeas and wash them properly. If you like, you can use them raw and skip to step 5.
  3. Chute the chickpeas into a bowl and mix them up with a bit of olive oil, salt and cayenne pepper, until all of them are properly covered in spices.
  4. Take the chickpeas into a pan, oven or airfryer and fry or bake them until they are crunchy.
  5. Cut the avocado in half and remove the core. Then use a spoon to get the flesh out of the shell. Cut one half of the Avocado into slices. Chop the other half into small pieces as these will used to make the sauce.
  6. Wash all the vegetables. Use a vegetable grater to grate the carrot and the Zucchini into stripes.
  7. Cut both of the cabages into stripes.
  8. Chop the tomatoes into halfs or quaters.
  9. take the vegetables and put them into a eating-bowl. You can eather organize them like in the picture, or mix them together. Top them with some salt and pepper.
  10. To make tha sauce, put the cubes avocado, the soy yoghurt and the other ingredients stated for the sauce into a blender and mix them, until they form a smooth cream. If you do not have a blender, put them into a bowl and smash the avocado with a fork, then mix in the other ingredients.
  11. After the noodles are cooked, rinse them and put them to the bowl with the vegetables.
  12. Put the fried chickpeas into the bowl.
  13. Top everything up with the avocado cream and ENJOY!
